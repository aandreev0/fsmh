# Controlling sample environment

Imaging live and fixed samples can require controlling temperature, humidity, gas composition, and ambient light levels around the sample. Simplest example is imaging cells where [ideally we want sample to be at 36C, 25% humidity and 5% CO2](https://journals.biologists.com/jcs/article/122/6/753/30735/Live-cell-microscopy-tips-and-tools?guestAccessKey=#6726759).

Two main approaches to maintaining environment exist: enclosing sample in a [compact box](https://oko-lab.com/live-cell-imaging/microscope-incubators/stagetop-incubator) with precise conditions inside, or surrounding the whole microscope (or large parts of it) with a [conditioned enclosure](https://www.elliotscientific.com/DPMH-Microscope-Incubators). Depending on system geometry and other requirements one might select one or the other solution.
